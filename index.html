The Presuppositions of NLP

These are the guiding beliefs that underpin all of NLP, not facts, but powerful assumptions. Hover or tap each card to reveal the principle.

01
The Map is Not the Territory

Presupposition
Our perception of reality is not reality itself. Every person experiences the world through their own unique filters and those filters shape everything.
02
People Have All the Resources They Need

Presupposition
Every person already has the internal resources to achieve their desired outcomes. NLP helps access and utilise these effectively.
03
Every Behaviour Has a Positive Intention
tap to reveal ↻
Presupposition
At some level, every behaviour serves a positive purpose for the individual. Understanding this creates compassion and opens pathways to change.
04
There Is No Failure, Only Feedback
tap to reveal ↻
Presupposition
Every outcome provides information. What others call failure, NLP practitioners call feedback, valuable data to adjust and improve.
05
Respect Others' Models of the World
tap to reveal ↻
Presupposition
Each person's worldview is valid for them. Respecting, not judging, another person's model is central to building genuine rapport.
06
The Meaning of Communication Is the Response
tap to reveal ↻
Presupposition
Regardless of your intention, the true meaning of communication is the response it produces. If it's not working, change your approach.
07
Mind and Body Are One System
tap to reveal ↻
Presupposition
The mind and body continuously influence each other. A change in one always produces a change in the other, NLP uses this connection powerfully.
08
If One Person Can Do It, Anyone Can Learn It

Presupposition
Excellence can be modelled. Any skill demonstrated by a human being can be studied, understood and replicated through NLP modelling.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

What is NLP and why does it matter? +
Neuro Linguistic Programming is best described as the instruction manual your mind never came with. At a technical level, it is the study of how your neurology, language and habitual thinking patterns shape every result you produce in life. At a human level, it is the art of understanding why you do what you do, and learning how to change it, fast. Where most personal development asks you to think differently, NLP shows you exactly how to rewire the patterns driving your thoughts, emotions and behaviour so that lasting change becomes not just possible, but inevitable.
How is certification possible in such a short time? +
Our Practitioner training uses an internationally accredited accelerated learning format. The full programme involves approximately 135 hours of study including the live training days. We do offer Pre-training Live Online Sessions when a client initially signs up. This forms part of the Preparation phase leading up to the live in-person training and is led by a Connect2 highly skilled Trainer. There is no cost for this. A trainer consultation fee only applies for extra pre-training sessions under the Re-Run option, and only if the client requests this.
Can I really receive 4 certifications from one programme? +
Yes. The Practitioner programme awards four internationally recognised certifications: NLP Practitioner, Time Line Therapy®, NLP Coaching, and Hypnotherapy, all accredited by the American Board of NLP, the Time Line Therapy® Association, and the American Board of Hypnotherapy. The Master Practitioner then builds on all four at a deeper, more advanced level.
Are there different levels of training available? +
Yes. We offer NLP Practitioner, NLP Master Practitioner, and Trainer's Training at the highest level. Each level deepens your skill, expands your certification, and opens new professional possibilities, whether you are working on yourself, coaching others, or building a practice or business around this work.
Will my qualifications be internationally recognised? +
Absolutely. All Connect2 certifications are accredited by the American Board of NLP (ABNLP), the Coaching Division of the ABNLP, the Time Line Therapy® Association (TLA), the American Board of Hypnotherapy (ABH), and the International Federation of Neuro Linguistic Programming (IFNLP). These are the most respected accreditation bodies in the NLP world and your certifications carry professional weight globally. We have also certified many individuals in the USA and Australia, and have actively collaborated with the well-known Dr Tad James for several years, certifying many students together.
What can I do with an NLP certification? +
The applications are wide-ranging. Graduates go on to establish coaching or therapy practices, deliver corporate training and leadership programmes, work with athletes and performance-focused individuals, support schools and educators, and accelerate their own careers. Many come simply to reshape their personal lives, and leave with the tools to elevate others too.
How long has Connect2 been operating? +
Connect2 was established in 1998. Our team brings over 80 years of combined professional experience in NLP, Time Line Techniques, Coaching and Hypnosis. We have trained thousands of individuals across South Africa, the United Kingdom, Puerto Rico, and beyond.
Who attends Connect2 training? +
Our graduates come from every walk of life, including coaches, therapists, executives, entrepreneurs, educators, health professionals, athletes, and individuals simply ready for meaningful change. What they all share is one thing: a genuine willingness to do the work. NLP is not a passive experience. The trainer is there to teach it all in depth, but real growth requires full commitment and energy from each delegate.
Who are my trainers? +
All Connect2 trainers are certified at the highest international levels, as ABNLP Master Trainers in NLP, Hypnosis and Time Line Therapy®. With over 80 years of combined experience between them, they bring deep expertise and a genuinely personal approach to every room. Visit our Team section to meet each trainer and see who is delivering in your region.
How many people will be in my training group? +
We typically work with groups of up to 20 participants, and we assign one qualified coaching assistant for every six delegates throughout the training. This ensures every person receives the depth of attention, practice time, and personal support that genuine breakthroughs require, not just theory delivered to a crowd.
Do you offer online training? +
Yes. We offer fully live online training for those who cannot attend in person. Our online programmes deliver the same internationally accredited curriculum, the same standard of trainer, and the same certification outcomes as our live in-person events. We also run regular online webinars and workshops as an introduction to NLP for those exploring their options.
Is there support after I graduate? +
Yes, graduation is the beginning, not the end. You join the C2 alumni community, gain access to ongoing webinars, accountability groups, and the C2 Zone where your learning continues. Our trainers remain accessible and we are committed to your growth long after the final day of training.
What is the Connect2 Re-Run policy? +
We believe NLP is lived, not just learned. Any graduate may return to repeat a training they have previously completed, as many times as they like, at zero cost, within a five-year period. This is subject to availability and is designed to help you deepen your practice over time. No refund will apply, and where new or updated material is introduced, a small fee may apply for those additions. This offer is of course subject to the company continuing to operate.
Are payment plans available? +
Yes. A 50% deposit secures your seat on any certified training programme, with the balance due before the event. For certified trainings, arrangements for a 6-month payment plan may be discussed and agreed with our accounts team. Full payment is required for short courses. For corporate and group bookings, pricing, structure and payment terms are agreed in advance based on your specific requirements and number of delegates. Contact us to discuss what works best for you.
Do you work with corporate teams and organisations? +
Yes. We regularly work with corporate groups and organisations of all sizes. Training scope, content, duration and investment are tailored to your team's goals and agreed prior to the event. Whether you are looking to develop leadership capability, embed a coaching culture, or drive performance across your organisation, we build the programme around you. Reach out to start the conversation, or visit our Corporate & Business page to learn more.

What is Neuro-Linguistic
Programming?

NLP is a set of tools, techniques and principles that help people understand how their mind works, so they can change unhelpful thoughts, emotions and behaviours to achieve better results in life, work and relationships.

N
Neuro
Your neurological processes, how you think and use your senses to experience the world.
L
Linguistic
Your language and internal dialogue, how you communicate with yourself and with others.
P
Programming
Your learned patterns of behaviour and thinking, the "programs" running in your mind.
A Bit of History
Connect2 NLP, Watch and Discover

Developed in the 1970s in California by Richard Bandler & John Grinder

They studied highly effective therapists, including Milton Erickson, Virginia Satir and Fritz Perls, to model what made them so successful. Their central question was: "What makes the difference between someone who is average and someone who excels?"

The answer became the foundation of NLP, a practical, results-oriented framework that has since spread to every corner of coaching, leadership, therapy and personal development worldwide.

How NLP Works

NLP operates on the idea that our thoughts, language and behaviours are interconnected and can be deliberately changed to produce better results.

Subjective Experience

Every person creates their own "map" of reality based on what they see (Visual), hear (Auditory), feel (Kinesthetic) and to a lesser degree, smell and taste. NLP works with these filters to change how you experience the world.

Modelling Excellence

The original founders studied highly successful people and identified patterns in how they think, speak and behave. NLP "models" these patterns so others can replicate them.

Key Operating Principle

Change your internal representation of something → change your emotional state and behaviour. Your unconscious mind follows patterns. NLP gives you tools to update or upgrade those patterns.

Creating triggers for desired emotional states, so you can access resourceful feelings on demand.

Reframing

Changing the meaning you give to events, turning problems into possibilities.

Rapport Building

Swish Pattern

Replacing unwanted behaviours with desired ones through deliberate submodality shifts.

Time Line Therapy™

Releasing negative emotions from past events and limiting decisions for permanent change.

Visualisation / Future Pacing

Mentally rehearsing success so your unconscious mind can navigate toward your desired outcome.

The Success Mindset
+

The success mindset sets the foundation for achieving your personal goals. This module explores the internal frames that separate people who consistently achieve from those who do not. You will examine and begin to shift your own patterns around the following:

  • Cause & Effect — understanding whether you operate from a position of power or victimhood

3
Prime Directives of the Unconscious Mind
+

The unconscious mind is the seat of all emotion, habit and automatic behaviour. Its primary functions are to store and organise memories and to maintain the integrity and wellbeing of the body. Understanding how the unconscious operates — and how to work with it rather than against it — is one of the most profound insights NLP offers. In this module you will discover the key directives that drive behaviour beneath your awareness and learn how to align your unconscious with your conscious goals.

4
The NLP Model of Communication
+

Before experience is processed by the conscious mind, it passes through a rich set of neurological and linguistic filters. These filters impose deletions, distortions and generalisations on everything we perceive, creating our unique internal map of reality. NLP pioneer John Grinder referred to raw sensory experience before conscious labelling as first access. Understanding this model reveals why two people can experience the same event entirely differently — and gives you the tools to expand your own map of the world.

5
Clarity, Direction & SMART Goals
+

This module takes you through a structured process of personal discovery and goal-setting. You will work through three interconnected stages:

  • Clarity & Direction — Discover your needs and explore what you truly want. Which area of your life is calling for change right now? What does the life you want actually look like?
  • Action & Momentum — What specific actions will move you toward your goals? What tasks are within your control and can be started immediately?
  • Break The Barriers — Identify the mental limitations that have been holding you back and learn how to turn them into a driving force rather than a ceiling.
  • SMART Goals — Learn to set goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant and Time-bound, so that your intentions become real, trackable milestones.

You will leave this session with a clear 3-month, 6-month and 12-month personal action plan.

6
Values & The Dickens Process
+

Sociologist Morris Massey identified three major periods during which our core values are formed:

  • The Imprint Period (0–7) — The formative years in which we develop our fundamental sense of right and wrong, good and bad, safe and unsafe.
  • The Modelling Period (8–13) — The years in which we consciously and unconsciously model parents, teachers and family members.
  • The Socialisation Period (14–21) — The period in which peer influence becomes dominant and our social identity begins to crystallise.

The Dickens Process — Named after Charles Dickens' A Christmas Carol, this powerful NLP process helps you identify the beliefs that have been limiting you and systematically replace them with beliefs that serve, empower and liberate you instead.

7
Representational Systems (V · K · A · Ad)
+

We each have a preferred way of processing and making sense of the world. NLP identifies four primary representational systems:

  • Visual (V) — Visual people think in pictures and place high importance on appearance, presentation and visual organisation. They often speak quickly and process information rapidly.
  • Kinesthetic (K) — Kinesthetic people process through feeling and physical sensation. They move at a slower pace, need to experience things in practice to fully learn them, and tend to be deeply in touch with their emotions.
  • Auditory (A) — Auditory people learn and process primarily through sound. They respond strongly to tone of voice, rhythm and music, and often prefer listening to reading.
  • Auditory Digital (Ad) — The auditory digital person spends significant time in internal self-talk. They need things to "make sense" logically before they will commit to them and may display characteristics of all the other systems at different times.

Understanding your own representational system — and that of others — is a foundational skill for communication, coaching and leadership.
